Sun

Approximate Diameter: 1,391,000 kilometers

Fun Fact: The Sun is a massive, glowing sphere of hot gas, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.

Mercury

Approximate Diameter: 4,880 kilometers

Fun Fact: Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system and the closest to the Sun.

Venus

Approximate Diameter: 12,104 kilometers

Fun Fact: Venus is often called Earth's sister planet because of their similar size and composition. It has a thick atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide, which traps heat and makes it the hottest planet in the solar system.

Earth

Approximate Diameter: 12,742 kilometers

Fun Fact: Earth is the only known planet to support life. It has a diverse range of ecosystems, including oceans, forests, deserts, and polar regions.

Mars

Approximate Diameter: 6,779 kilometers

Fun Fact: Mars is often called the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance caused by iron oxide on its surface. It has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and a giant canyon system called Valles Marineris.

Jupiter

Approximate Diameter: 139,820 kilometers

Fun Fact: Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and is known for its massive size and iconic swirling cloud patterns, including the Great Red Spot, a massive storm that has been raging for centuries.

Saturn

Approximate Diameter: 116,460 kilometers

Fun Fact: Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system, which is made up of icy particles and debris. It is the second-largest planet in the solar system and has a relatively low density, which means it could float in water if you could find a body of water large enough.

Uranus

Approximate Diameter: 50,724 kilometers

Fun Fact: Uranus is unique among the planets because it rotates on its side, likely due to a collision early in its formation. It has a pale blue color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere.

Neptune

Approximate Diameter: 49,244 kilometers

Fun Fact: Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is known for its deep blue color. It has strong winds and storms, including the Great Dark Spot, similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot but smaller and more fleeting.
